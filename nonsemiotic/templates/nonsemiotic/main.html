{% trans 'About our project' %}

{% trans 'The project is dedicated to the typology of constructions that describe spontaneous movements of parts of the body (compare "raise your head", "stand on one’s feet" or "wag the tail").' %}

{% trans 'There are numerous descriptions of semiotically loaded gestures, such as "wringing one’s hands", "pouting one’s lips", "nodding one’s head", etc. (cf. Kreidlin 2002, 2013, Kreidlin, Letuchy 2006). However, semiotics is culture specific, which makes it difficult to build a typology on this material: the same movement of the head can mean YES in one language (as in English), but NO in another (as in Bulgarian).' %}

{% trans 'Unlike semiotic gestures, non-semiotic ones present an attractive task for linguistic typology. The subject is also appealing because it touches upon several well-studied areas, such as the typology of verbs of motion (cf. Talmy 1985, 2000, Slobin 2004), the typology of body parts (cf. Andersen 1978, Koch 2008, Majid 2010), and the typology of causative constructions (Comrie, Polinsky 1993, Song 2001). At the same time, for each of these areas our constructions are peripheral and thus are rarely in the focus of attention of researchers.' %}
